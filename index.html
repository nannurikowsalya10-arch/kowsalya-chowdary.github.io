About

Myself

I'm a Data Engineer focused on ML and AI, currently building production-grade data and AI pipelines at MilliporeSigma.

My work sits at the intersection of large-scale data systems and applied machine learning, turning messy operational data into reliable datasets, metrics, and decision-ready models.

I've shipped enterprise data platforms across finance and global economic institutions — including SMBC Group and the International Monetary Fund — using Databricks-based ETL, PySpark, SQL, and Cassandra-backed pipeline workflows.

I'm increasingly drawn to agentic AI and LLM-powered workflows — using retrieval-augmented generation, embeddings, and tool-orchestration patterns to make data pipelines smarter and more self-serving.

What I care about most is building things that hold up under real-world pressure — pipelines that don't break under scale, and architectures that teams can actually rely on when business decisions ride on them.

Having worked across enterprise life sciences, global banking, and an international economic institution, I've learned that good data engineering looks a little different in every context — but the discipline behind it stays the same.
